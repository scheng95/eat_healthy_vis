How can we eat better?






What is healthy?

Confused? So is everyone. Generally, eating more healthy means spending as much energy as you eat, eating a variety of nutritious foods and limiting empty calories.

Why does it matter?

Over 2/3 of American adults are overweight or obese. Heart disease, stroke, and diabetes are among the top ten causes of death, and they are all diet-related. See how the average American is eating.

What can you do?

Get to know your food better. Studies have shown that eating at home has higher nutrition density than eating out at restaurants. Figure out what to make with our nutritional analysis tool.

What is Healthy?


Over the years, nutrition experts have stayed consistent on two recommendations:


Calories In = Calories Out

If you consume more energy than you spend, you'll gain weight. Maintain a balance to maintain a healthy weight.

Not All Calories are Equal

You need enough of the good nutrients (vitamins, minerals, etc) and not too much of the bad (sugar, fats, cholesterol, sodium)

How healthy are Americans?


In general, people overconsume in calories, sugar, and sodium, and don't get enough fiber.
